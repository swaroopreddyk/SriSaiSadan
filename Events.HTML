Daily Events

Abhisekham / Puja

Every morning after the Kakad Aarathi, sacred bath is undertaken for baba. On Thursdays panchamrutha abhisekham is performed. After the bath, Maharaj is readied for darshan. Puja activity begins with prayers to Lord Ganesh, Lord Dattatreya and then to Saibaba.

Dhuni Pooja

At 11:30 AM, priests of the temple perform the Dhuni pooja. Since the inception of the temple, Dhuni is maintained. Udi from the Dhuni is given to devotees. Saibaba used to sit in front of Dhuni and maintained it throughout his stay in shirdi.

Satcharitra Paaraayan

Every evening between 7:30 and 7:45 pm, one chapter from Satcharitra or Gurucharitra is studied by the devotees. Baba during his stay at shirdi, always advised his devotees to read religious scriptures..

Lullaby

Everyday after the Shejaarthi, devotees sing the lullaby for Baba. This lullaby was written in specific to this temple only. After this programme the closing of temple is initiated.

Vishnu / Lalitha Sahasranaam

Every evening from 6:45 pm, devotees chant the Vishnu sahasranaam. On Friday, Lalitha sahasranaam is also studied by the devotees. Over the centuries, it has been a practice in the Hindu mythology to study these scriptures. Baba also advocated this in Shirdi.

Aarthis

Four aarthis are conducted in the temple. Everyday at 6:15 am is Kakad aarthi (on thursdays it is at 5:30 am); Noon Aarthi is performed at 12:00 pm; Evening Aarthi is at 6:16 pm and Shejaarthi is at 9:00 pm (on thursday it is at 9:30 pm). Devotees chant the aarthi during this seva..

Sai Bhajans

From Monday to Saturday between 7:30 and 8:45 pm, devotees assemble in the temple hall and do bhajans of Sai, Lord Siva and Lord Vishnu. On Saturday, singers are encouraged to sing religious songs. On Sundays, devotees participate in Satsangam. Sai Maharaj always encouraged the devotees to perform in Dwarakamayi during his regime..

Weekly Events

Palki Seva

This is a must to watch seva at this temple. Every Thursday evening between 7:30 to 9:30 pm, the palanquin decorated with beautiful flowers and embroidered cloth, is used to carry small marble idol of Sai Maharaj along with the padukas and satka. Devotees first go in procession to Lord Ganesh temple and then round the temple. Devotees chant sai naam accompanied with band and music.

Satsangam

Every Sunday evening from 7:30 pm, Sri Allu Bhaskar Reddy delivers lectures on Sai Baba, Lord Dattatreya and other religious discourses. Devotees are enlightened about the life of Saibaba and his mode of living

Annual Events

Sri Ramanavami

A must to watch festival at the temple. At Sai Sadan, this festival is celebrated for 3 days with great show and pomp. The celebrations begin with Abhisekham and chanting of Sainaamam. In the morning pujas are performed. In the evening sandal procession takes place. The procession starts from a Darga, where muslims & hindus sing the holy songsand offer prayers at the Darga. After the prayers, Sandal paste and other items are carried by the devotees on their heads and the procession is taken along the streets of Balaji Nagar in Nellore. After the procession reaches the temple, prayers are offered at Sai Sadan and the sandal paste is put on the walls of the temple and distributed to the devotees. Dances by muslims, prayers by muslim priests, pandari bhajan, beautiful decorated horse, chanting of sai naam are a pleasure to watch for any in this procession. This is one leading example for perfect communal harmony. Feeding of the poor is also undertaken in a large scale.

Dasara

This is the day when Sai Baba took his mahasamadhi. To commemorate this people light oil lamps in the temple during afternoon. Temple also maintains two "Akhanda Deepams". Two oil lamps continuously keep glowing in the temple through out the year. On this day, the lamps are taken, pooja is performed and kept back in the same position. In the morning Rudrabhisekhams, Prayers are performed. Palki procession is taken round the temple on this festival duing the evening. Next day, feeding to the poor is undertaken in a grand scale..

Guru Purnima

One of the most important festival that highlights the importance of Guru. It has been dated back to centuries where the role of Guru has been more prevalent in Indian heritage and culture. On this day at Sai Sadan, the complete study of Satcharitra and Guru Charitra is performed by the devotees. Sai Satya Vrata Puja is undertaken by the devotees. Feeding of the poor is also handled on a grander scale..

Deepavali

Widely celebrated festival in India. On Diwali day, Lakshmi pooja is done in the temple. During evening, devotees also fire crackers on this occasion..

Other Religion Festivals

At Sai Sadan, religious harmony is maintained. Important festivals of other religions are also conducted at the temple. During Ramzan, muslim clergies are invited to read Holy Quran and On Christmas, Father from the Church comes and studies Holy Bible. Atmosphere is also created in the temple to suit the festivals. Every important festival or event of any religion is celebrated with equal flavour..

Temple Anniversary

Every year on May 12, the anniversary of the temple is celebrated. Feeding of the poor is done on a grand scale..
